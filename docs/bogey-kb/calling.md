# Calling in Bulldog Chat

## Voice and video calls

### Calling someone directly

1. Open a DM with the person.
2. Click the **phone** icon (voice) or **camera** icon (video) at the top.
3. They'll get a ringing notification. When they answer, the call starts.

### Calling multiple people (group call)

1. Open a group DM or channel.
2. Click **Huddle** at the top.
3. Anyone in the conversation can join. Nobody is force-called — they see a notification and can hop in.

## Outbound phone calls

Bulldog can dial external phone numbers over Twilio.

1. Click **+ New call** in the sidebar.
2. Enter the phone number (US format: +1XXXXXXXXXX).
3. Optionally add a name so it appears in your call history.
4. Click **Call**.

Your call routes through your assigned Bulldog phone number. Callers see that number, not your personal cell.

## Inbound calls

If your workspace has phone numbers configured:
- Incoming calls ring in the Bulldog app.
- You can accept, decline, or send to voicemail.
- Missed calls appear in the **Calls** sidebar with the caller ID.

## Voicemail

- Callers who don't get an answer leave a voicemail.
- Voicemails appear as audio files in your **Calls** tab.
- Transcripts are auto-generated within a few seconds.

## Call recording

If your workspace enables call recording:
- Recording starts automatically on connect (with a beep to notify all parties).
- Recording ends when the call ends.
- Recordings appear in the call history with a play button and transcript.

## Call quality

- **Green** — good connection
- **Yellow** — degraded audio; try switching networks
- **Red** — likely to drop; call may end soon

## Blocking numbers

- Right-click a number in **Calls** → **Block**.
- Blocked numbers can't reach your Bulldog number.

## SMS to callers

From the **Calls** view, you can send an SMS to any number you've called or received a call from. Great for following up with a text after a voicemail.

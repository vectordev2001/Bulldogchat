# Notifications in Bulldog Chat

## Notification types

- **@mentions** — Someone tags you directly. Always notified by default.
- **Channel messages** — Regular posts in channels you're in.
- **DMs** — Direct messages to you or a group you're in.
- **Meeting reminders** — 5 minutes before a scheduled meeting.
- **Incoming calls** — Ring in the app and, optionally, push to your phone.
- **SMS** — If your workspace has phone numbers, SMS conversations show up like DMs.

## Global notification settings

1. Click your avatar (top left) → **Preferences**.
2. Go to **Notifications**.

Options:
- **All new messages** — every message in every channel
- **Direct messages, mentions, and keywords** — quieter default
- **Nothing** — do not disturb

You can also set:
- Working hours (only notify during these hours)
- Do not disturb windows (mute for a set time)
- Sound and desktop banner preferences

## Per-channel notifications

Right-click any channel or DM → **Notification preferences**:
- Default (uses your global setting)
- All new messages
- Mentions only
- Nothing

## Keywords

Add words that should always ping you (project names, your first name, product terms):

1. Preferences → Notifications → **Keywords**.
2. Add one per line.
3. Bulldog will notify you when any of these appear in channels you're in.

## Mobile push notifications

- Install the Bulldog iOS or Android app.
- Sign in with the same account.
- Push notifications follow the same rules as desktop unless you set mobile-specific overrides.

## SMS consent

To receive Bulldog SMS notifications (meeting reminders, urgent alerts) on your phone:

1. Go to Preferences → **Phone & SMS**.
2. Add your phone number.
3. Confirm the verification code sent by SMS.
4. Choose which alerts should also come by SMS.

You can opt out at any time by texting STOP to the Bulldog number, or by toggling off in Preferences.

## Do Not Disturb

- Click your avatar → **Pause notifications**.
- Choose 30 min, 1 hour, until tomorrow, or custom.
- Others see a DND icon next to your name — they can still message but you won't be pinged.
